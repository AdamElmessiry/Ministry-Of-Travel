About the Ministry

The Egyptian Ministry of Tourism is the official body responsible for promoting, regulating, and developing Egypt’s tourism sector. Tourism is one of the country’s most vital industries, contributing significantly to economic growth, cultural exchange, and job creation.

From the timeless Pyramids of Giza to the tranquil beaches of the Red Sea, Egypt’s diverse attractions continue to inspire millions of travelers each year. Our ministry works to ensure that this journey is safe, memorable, and enriching for all.

History of Egyptian Tourism

Tourism in Egypt dates back thousands of years, with ancient travelers visiting the Nile Valley to marvel at its temples and monuments. In modern times, Egypt became one of the first countries in the region to establish a tourism authority, recognizing its importance to both cultural diplomacy and the national economy.

Today, the Ministry continues this legacy by preserving historical treasures while embracing innovation and modern hospitality standards.

Our Mission

Our mission is to highlight Egypt’s ancient wonders, natural beauty, and vibrant culture while supporting local communities and ensuring sustainability for future generations.

We aim to make Egypt one of the world’s most attractive, accessible, and sustainable destinations for travelers from all backgrounds.

Tourism in Numbers

Egypt welcomes millions of visitors each year, making it one of the top destinations in the Middle East and Africa. Tourism contributes more than 10% of Egypt’s GDP and provides livelihoods for millions of Egyptians across diverse sectors.

  • 100+ Museums and Archaeological Sites
  • 2,000 km of Coastline along the Red Sea and Mediterranean
  • 300+ Annual Cultural and Religious Festivals
  • 20+ UNESCO World Heritage Sites

Visitor Guidance

The Ministry of Tourism is committed to ensuring that every visitor enjoys Egypt safely and responsibly. We encourage all travelers to:

  • Respect cultural traditions and local customs.
  • Support local businesses and communities.
  • Follow eco-friendly practices during their travels.
  • Use official resources for information and assistance.
