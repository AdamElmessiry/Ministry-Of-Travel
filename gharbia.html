About Gharbia

Gharbia Governorate, located in the heart of the Nile Delta, is known for its agriculture, textile industry, and vibrant cultural life. Its capital, Tanta, is a major religious and cultural center, attracting thousands of visitors each year to its famous Sufi festivals. The governorate is also renowned for cotton production and traditional crafts.

Top Attractions

  • Tanta Mosque (Sayid Ahmad al-Badawi Mosque) — the center of Egypt’s largest Sufi festival.
  • Tanta Museum — showcasing antiquities from Pharaonic to Islamic periods.
  • Nile Delta Villages — traditional farming communities with lush landscapes.
  • Local Textile Markets — famous for cotton fabrics and traditional weaving.
  • Annual Moulid Festival — a vibrant religious celebration attracting pilgrims nationwide.

Visitor Info

Gharbia is about 90 km north of Cairo and accessible by train, bus, or car. The best time to visit is during the autumn festival season in Tanta or in spring, when the Delta countryside is especially beautiful. Travelers can enjoy authentic Egyptian rural culture alongside historic landmarks.
