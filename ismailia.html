About Ismailia

Ismailia Governorate lies on the west bank of the Suez Canal and is often called the “City of Beauty and Enchantment.” Established during the construction of the Suez Canal in the 19th century, the city is known for its colonial architecture, wide green spaces, and rich history. It serves as a strategic hub linking Africa and Asia.

Top Attractions

  • Suez Canal Authority Building — a historic landmark from the canal’s construction.
  • Ismailia Museum — showcasing Pharaonic, Greek, and Roman artifacts found in the canal region.
  • Lake Timsah — a popular spot for fishing, boating, and waterfront relaxation.
  • Public Gardens and Parks — famous for lush greenery and palm-lined boulevards.
  • Canal-side Corniche — a scenic promenade ideal for evening walks.

Visitor Info

Ismailia is about 120 km northeast of Cairo and is accessible via the Cairo-Ismailia Desert Road. The city is best visited in spring and autumn (March–May, September–November), when the weather is mild. Visitors often enjoy seafood restaurants by Lake Timsah and excursions along the canal. Annual cultural festivals also highlight the governorate’s artistic spirit.
