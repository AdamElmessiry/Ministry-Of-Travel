About Minya

Minya Governorate, located in Upper Egypt along the Nile River, is often referred to as the “Bride of Upper Egypt.” It is rich in Pharaonic, Greco-Roman, Coptic, and Islamic heritage, making it a living museum of Egyptian history. Minya also boasts fertile agricultural land, vibrant local culture, and charming riverfront views.

Top Attractions

  • Beni Hasan Tombs — rock-cut tombs from the Middle Kingdom with vivid wall paintings.
  • Tuna El-Gebel — archaeological site with ancient catacombs and the Tomb of Petosiris.
  • Tell el-Amarna — ruins of the capital city of Pharaoh Akhenaten and Queen Nefertiti.
  • Monastery of the Virgin Mary (Gabal el-Teir) — an important Christian pilgrimage site.
  • The Nile Corniche — a scenic riverside promenade in Minya city.

Visitor Info

Minya is about 245 km south of Cairo and can be reached by train, bus, or car. The best time to visit is between October and April, when the weather is cooler and ideal for exploring archaeological sites. Visitors can also enjoy boat rides on the Nile, local cuisine, and a warm, hospitable atmosphere in the governorate’s towns and villages.
