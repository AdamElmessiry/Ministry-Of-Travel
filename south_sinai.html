About South Sinai

South Sinai Governorate covers the southern half of the Sinai Peninsula and is famous for its dramatic mountains, deserts, and Red Sea coastline. It is a major tourist destination, offering diving, hiking, and eco-tourism. The governorate also holds religious and historical significance, including Saint Catherine’s Monastery and Mount Sinai.

Top Attractions

  • Sharm El-Sheikh — a premier resort city for diving, beaches, and luxury accommodations.
  • Mount Sinai — the biblical mountain for hiking and sunrise experiences.
  • Saint Catherine’s Monastery — a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the oldest Christian monasteries in the world.
  • Ras Mohammed National Park — offering coral reefs, snorkeling, and desert wildlife.
  • Blue Hole — a world-famous diving site near Dahab.

Visitor Info

South Sinai is accessible via Sharm El-Sheikh International Airport, Taba, and road networks from Cairo. The best time to visit is from October to April, when temperatures are comfortable for both desert and coastal activities. Visitors can enjoy water sports, desert treks, and cultural tours in a region rich in natural beauty and history.
