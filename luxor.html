About Luxor

Luxor Governorate is one of the world’s greatest open-air museums, located on the east and west banks of the Nile. It is home to some of the most remarkable monuments of ancient Egypt, including temples, tombs, and statues that date back thousands of years. Luxor attracts millions of visitors annually and is a central pillar of Egypt’s tourism industry.

Top Attractions

  • Karnak Temple Complex — a vast complex of temples dedicated to the Theban gods.
  • Luxor Temple — located in the city center, especially stunning at night.
  • Valley of the Kings — the burial site of famous pharaohs, including Tutankhamun.
  • Valley of the Queens — home to the beautifully decorated tomb of Queen Nefertari.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides — offering breathtaking views of the Nile and monuments at sunrise.

Visitor Info

Luxor is about 670 km south of Cairo and accessible by plane, train, or cruise on the Nile. The best time to visit is between October and April, when temperatures are mild. Visitors should plan multiple days to fully experience both the East and West Bank. Luxor also hosts cultural festivals and traditional markets that enrich the travel experience.
