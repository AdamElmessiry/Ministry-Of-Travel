About Cairo

Cairo, Egypt’s bustling capital, is a city where ancient history meets modern vibrance. Known as “The City of a Thousand Minarets,” it is home to some of the most iconic landmarks in the world, including the Pyramids of Giza and the Sphinx. Beyond its monuments, Cairo is a cultural and economic hub, alive with markets, museums, and lively neighborhoods.

Top Attractions

  • The Pyramids of Giza — timeless wonders of the ancient world.
  • The Egyptian Museum — home to treasures of Tutankhamun and other pharaohs.
  • Khan El-Khalili Bazaar — a lively market filled with crafts, spices, and souvenirs.
  • Islamic Cairo — historic mosques, madrassas, and cultural landmarks.
  • Old Cairo — Coptic churches, including the Hanging Church.

Visitor Info

Cairo is accessible year-round, though the best time to visit is from October to April, when the weather is cooler. Transportation includes metro, taxis, and ride-sharing. Visitors should explore both the historic areas and the modern districts for a complete experience.
