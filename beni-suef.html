About Beni Suef

Beni Suef, located south of Cairo along the Nile, is a governorate known for its agriculture, heritage, and developing industries. The region has been historically significant since ancient times, with ties to the Middle Kingdom of Egypt. Today, Beni Suef is both a cultural and economic hub, blending history with modern growth.

Top Attractions

  • Meidum Pyramid — an early step pyramid built for Pharaoh Sneferu.
  • Ihnasya Ruins (Heracleopolis Magna) — remains of an important ancient city.
  • Beni Suef Museum — exhibits covering Pharaonic to Islamic history.
  • Nile Corniche — a pleasant riverside area for evening walks.
  • Local Handicrafts — traditional pottery and weaving from local villages.

Visitor Info

Beni Suef lies about 120 km south of Cairo, reachable by car, train, or bus. The best time to visit is from November to March, when the weather is cooler. Travelers can explore ancient sites during the day and enjoy the relaxed Nile-side atmosphere in the evenings.
