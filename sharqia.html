About Sharqia

Sharqia Governorate is located in the eastern Nile Delta and is one of Egypt’s most fertile agricultural regions. Known for its fields of rice, wheat, and cotton, Sharqia plays a vital role in Egypt’s food production. It also has a long history dating back to the Pharaonic period and is home to several archaeological sites and rural traditions.

Top Attractions

  • Zagazig — the capital city, hosting universities and cultural centers.
  • Tall el-Amarna Ruins — archaeological remains linked to Egypt’s ancient past.
  • Belbeis — an old town with Islamic landmarks and historic mosques.
  • Nile Branch Landscapes — offering views of canals, fields, and village life.
  • Local Festivals — traditional celebrations highlighting Sharqia’s rural culture.

Visitor Info

Sharqia is located about 80 km northeast of Cairo, making it easy to reach by road. The best time to visit is during spring or autumn, when the weather is mild and the fields are green. Visitors can explore agricultural life, enjoy local markets, and learn about the governorate’s historical importance.
