About Asyut

Asyut, located in central Egypt along the Nile, is one of the country’s oldest continuously inhabited regions. Known as “the heart of Upper Egypt,” it has been a hub for culture, education, and religion since Pharaonic times. The governorate boasts ancient tombs, monasteries, and a rich Coptic and Islamic heritage.

Top Attractions

  • Meir Tombs — rock-cut tombs dating back to the Old and Middle Kingdoms.
  • Monastery of Saint Mary (Deir el-Muharraq) — an important Coptic Christian pilgrimage site.
  • Asyut Museum — showcasing Pharaonic, Greek, and Roman artifacts.
  • Old Mosques — including the historic Mosque of Asyut.
  • Nile Corniche — a peaceful riverside promenade perfect for evening strolls.

Visitor Info

Asyut is around 375 km south of Cairo and can be reached by train, bus, or car. The best time to visit is in winter and early spring (November to March), when the weather is pleasant. Travelers can combine Asyut with trips to nearby governorates for a deeper exploration of Upper Egypt.
