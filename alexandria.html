About Alexandria

Alexandria, founded by Alexander the Great in 331 BC, is Egypt’s second-largest city and a cultural jewel on the Mediterranean coast. Known as the “Pearl of the Mediterranean,” it blends Greek, Roman, and Egyptian history. Today, it is a bustling port city with a unique cosmopolitan charm, filled with seaside cafes, museums, and historic landmarks.

Top Attractions

  • Bibliotheca Alexandrina — a modern library inspired by the ancient Library of Alexandria.
  • Qaitbay Citadel — a 15th-century fortress built on the site of the ancient Lighthouse.
  • Montaza Palace Gardens — lush royal gardens by the sea.
  • Pompey’s Pillar — a towering Roman triumphal column.
  • Alexandria National Museum — showcasing the city’s diverse history.

Visitor Info

Alexandria is located about 225 km northwest of Cairo and can be reached by train, bus, or car. The best time to visit is spring (March–May) or autumn (September–November), when the weather is mild. The Corniche offers stunning sea views, and seafood restaurants are among the best in Egypt.
