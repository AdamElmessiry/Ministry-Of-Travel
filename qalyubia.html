About Qalyubia

Qalyubia Governorate is part of Greater Cairo and lies directly north of the capital. It is an important agricultural and industrial hub, producing vegetables, fruit, and cotton while also hosting factories and workshops. Qalyubia blends urban expansion with rural traditions and has a strategic position connecting Cairo to the Delta.

Top Attractions

  • Banha — the capital, known for its bustling markets and historical sites.
  • Banha University — a cultural and educational landmark of the governorate.
  • Shibin El-Qanater — a historic town with old bridges and waterworks on the Nile branches.
  • Delta Countryside — offering scenic agricultural landscapes.
  • Local Mosques and Churches — reflecting Egypt’s religious and cultural diversity.

Visitor Info

Qalyubia is just 30 km north of Cairo, making it an easy day trip by road or train. The best time to visit is in spring or autumn, when the countryside is especially green. Visitors can explore rural traditions, shop in local markets, and enjoy authentic Egyptian food in family-run restaurants.
