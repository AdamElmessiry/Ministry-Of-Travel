About Suez

Suez Governorate is located at the southern entrance of the Suez Canal and is a key maritime and industrial hub of Egypt. The governorate has a strategic position connecting the Mediterranean and Red Sea, and it features a mix of modern port facilities, historical landmarks, and desert landscapes. Suez plays a critical role in trade, transportation, and Egypt’s economic activities.

Top Attractions

  • Suez Canal — observe ships navigating one of the world’s busiest waterways.
  • Port Tawfiq — the city’s main port with historical significance.
  • Al Arbaeen Mosque — a local Islamic landmark of cultural importance.
  • Suez Corniche — a scenic waterfront promenade along the Red Sea.
  • Desert Excursions — explore nearby mountains and desert landscapes.

Visitor Info

Suez is about 130 km northeast of Cairo and is accessible via road or train. The best time to visit is in winter and spring, when temperatures are comfortable. Travelers can explore port areas, waterfront views, and nearby desert landscapes. The city is also a convenient stop for those exploring the Suez Canal region.
