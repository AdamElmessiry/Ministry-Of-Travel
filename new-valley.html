About New Valley

The New Valley Governorate (Al Wadi Al Gedid) is the largest in Egypt by area, covering much of the Western Desert. Despite its vast desert landscape, it is dotted with oases, fertile farmlands, and historic sites. The governorate is rich in archaeological remains, natural springs, and eco-tourism opportunities, making it one of Egypt’s hidden treasures.

Top Attractions

  • Kharga Oasis — the capital of the governorate, known for its lush palm groves and hot springs.
  • Dakhla Oasis — home to ancient tombs, Islamic villages, and breathtaking desert scenery.
  • Farafra Oasis — famous for the White Desert with its surreal chalk rock formations.
  • Temple of Hibis — a well-preserved Persian-era temple in Kharga.
  • Traditional Desert Villages — offering authentic cultural experiences in remote settings.

Visitor Info

The New Valley is about 600 km southwest of Cairo, accessible by road and regional flights. The best time to visit is during winter (November–March), when temperatures are mild. Visitors should prepare for long travel distances but will be rewarded with stunning desert views, unique natural landmarks, and unforgettable stargazing opportunities.
