About Matrouh

Matrouh Governorate stretches along Egypt’s northwestern Mediterranean coast. It is renowned for its pristine beaches, turquoise waters, and unique desert landscapes. The governorate also carries historical importance, having witnessed pivotal events during World War II, and it continues to thrive as a major summer destination for local and international travelers.

Top Attractions

  • Marsa Matrouh Beach — famous for crystal-clear waters and soft sand.
  • Almaza Bay — a luxury resort destination with stunning coastline.
  • Siwa Oasis — a desert paradise with natural springs and ancient temples.
  • El-Alamein — historic WWII battle site with museums and cemeteries.
  • Cleopatra’s Rock and Bath — legendary sites linked to Queen Cleopatra.

Visitor Info

Marsa Matrouh, the capital, is about 500 km northwest of Cairo and can be reached by car, bus, or train. Summer (June–September) is the peak season, when Egyptians flock to the Mediterranean coast. For a more serene experience, visitors can explore Siwa Oasis, about 300 km inland, which offers eco-lodges, palm groves, and salt lakes. Matrouh combines beach relaxation, desert adventure, and history in one destination.
