About Giza

Giza Governorate, located on the west bank of the Nile across from Cairo, is one of Egypt’s most famous destinations. Home to the legendary Pyramids and the Great Sphinx, Giza is a global symbol of ancient civilization. Beyond its ancient wonders, the governorate also offers modern urban life, fertile farmland, and desert landscapes.

Top Attractions

  • The Great Pyramid of Khufu — the last remaining wonder of the ancient world.
  • The Great Sphinx — the iconic guardian statue of the Giza Plateau.
  • Pyramid of Khafre and Menkaure — part of the grand necropolis complex.
  • Solar Boat Museum — housing the reconstructed ancient pharaonic boat.
  • Sakkara and Dahshur — nearby necropolises with step and bent pyramids.

Visitor Info

Giza is easily accessible from Cairo and is a must-visit destination for all travelers to Egypt. The best time to visit is between October and April, when the weather is pleasant for exploring the desert. Visitors are advised to bring comfortable shoes, sunscreen, and water when touring the pyramids. Evening sound-and-light shows at the pyramids provide a spectacular way to experience the monuments.
