About Qena

Qena Governorate is located in Upper Egypt along the Nile River. It is a region with deep historical roots, known for its proximity to some of Egypt’s most famous archaeological treasures. The governorate combines agricultural wealth with a vibrant cultural heritage, and its capital, Qena City, is a growing urban center.

Top Attractions

  • Dendera Temple Complex — one of the best-preserved temples in Egypt, dedicated to Hathor, goddess of love and joy.
  • Nile River Corniche — offering peaceful walks and local cafés by the river.
  • Traditional Handicrafts — Qena is known for pottery, weaving, and artisanal goods.
  • Luxor Proximity — just north of Luxor, making it a perfect stop on a Nile journey.
  • Qena City Markets — colorful bazaars selling food, spices, and textiles.

Visitor Info

Qena lies about 600 km south of Cairo and can be reached by train, car, or Nile cruise. The best time to visit is between October and April, when the weather is cooler. Visitors can combine a trip to Qena with nearby Luxor and Aswan, exploring temples, tombs, and the timeless beauty of the Nile Valley.
