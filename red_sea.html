About Red Sea Governorate

The Red Sea Governorate stretches along Egypt’s eastern coast, facing the Red Sea. It is one of the country’s premier tourist destinations, known for its beaches, coral reefs, and luxury resorts. The governorate includes famous cities such as Hurghada, Marsa Alam, and Safaga, each offering sun, sea, and adventure. It is also a hub for diving, marine life exploration, and desert safaris.

Top Attractions

  • Hurghada — a bustling resort city with beaches, nightlife, and diving spots.
  • Marsa Alam — famous for dolphin houses, sea turtles, and pristine reefs.
  • Giftun Islands — popular for snorkeling and boat trips.
  • Desert Safaris — exploring mountains, oases, and Bedouin camps.
  • Safaga — known for windsurfing, kitesurfing, and mineral-rich beaches.

Visitor Info

The Red Sea Governorate is accessible via Hurghada International Airport and Marsa Alam Airport, as well as by road from Cairo (about 450 km). The best time to visit is year-round, though winter (November–March) offers the most pleasant temperatures. Travelers should not miss the chance to dive or snorkel in some of the world’s most stunning coral reefs.
