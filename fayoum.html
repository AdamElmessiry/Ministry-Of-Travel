About Faiyum

Faiyum Governorate, southwest of Cairo, is one of Egypt’s oldest regions, dating back to the Pharaonic era. Known for its lush fields, ancient monuments, and stunning desert landscapes, Faiyum offers a rare blend of nature and history. The governorate is also home to Lake Qarun, one of the oldest natural lakes in the world.

Top Attractions

  • Lake Qarun — ideal for fishing, birdwatching, and relaxation.
  • Wadi El-Rayan — Egypt’s only waterfalls, surrounded by desert scenery.
  • Wadi El-Hitan (Valley of the Whales) — a UNESCO site with prehistoric whale fossils.
  • Hawara Pyramid — built by Pharaoh Amenemhat III during the Middle Kingdom.
  • Tunis Village — famous for pottery workshops and arts festivals.

Visitor Info

Faiyum is around 100 km southwest of Cairo and is easily accessible by car or bus. The best time to visit is in winter and spring (November–April), when the weather is pleasant for exploring. Visitors can enjoy eco-lodges, desert safaris, and authentic local cuisine.
