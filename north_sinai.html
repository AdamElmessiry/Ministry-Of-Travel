About North Sinai

North Sinai Governorate lies along the Mediterranean coast in northeastern Egypt. Historically, it has been a land bridge between Africa and Asia, playing an important role in trade and cultural exchange. The region is known for its sandy beaches, Bedouin traditions, and archaeological remains. Despite its challenges, North Sinai remains an area of cultural richness and natural beauty.

Top Attractions

  • El-Arish — the capital city, featuring palm-lined beaches and traditional markets.
  • Ruins of Pelusium — an ancient city that once guarded Egypt’s eastern frontier.
  • Bedouin Camps — offering insight into traditional nomadic life and hospitality.
  • Mediterranean Coast — known for its serene beaches and fishing communities.
  • Sinai Desert Landscapes — vast dunes and mountain ranges ideal for exploration.

Visitor Info

North Sinai is approximately 344 km northeast of Cairo, reachable by road. Visitors are encouraged to explore local culture, sample Bedouin cuisine, and enjoy the Mediterranean climate. The best time to visit is spring or autumn, when the weather is pleasant. Travel advisories should be checked before visiting, as the region has occasionally faced security concerns.
