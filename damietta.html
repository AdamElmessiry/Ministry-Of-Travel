About Damietta

Damietta Governorate is located in the northeastern Nile Delta, where the river meets the Mediterranean Sea. It is famous for its furniture industry, seafood, and strategic coastal location. The governorate blends industry, agriculture, and natural beauty, making it a unique destination in Egypt.

Top Attractions

  • Ras El-Bar — a resort town at the meeting point of the Nile and Mediterranean.
  • Damietta Furniture Workshops — renowned worldwide for fine woodcraft.
  • Al-Burullus Lake (nearby) — a natural haven for fishing and birdwatching.
  • El-Lesan Peninsula — the exact spot where river and sea converge.
  • Seafood Markets — offering the freshest catches from the Mediterranean.

Visitor Info

Damietta is about 200 km northeast of Cairo, reachable by car, train, or bus. The best time to visit is spring (March–May) or autumn (September–November), when the climate is mild. Travelers should not miss Ras El-Bar’s beaches and the city’s famous seafood dishes.
