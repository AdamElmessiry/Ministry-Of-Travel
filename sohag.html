About Sohag

Sohag Governorate is located in Upper Egypt along the Nile Valley. Known for its agricultural richness and archaeological treasures, Sohag has roots that stretch back to ancient Egypt. The governorate is home to Pharaonic temples, Coptic monasteries, and Islamic landmarks, offering a rich blend of cultural and religious history. Its capital, Sohag City, is an important administrative and commercial center.

Top Attractions

  • Abydos — the ancient cult center of Osiris, home to the famous Temple of Seti I.
  • White Monastery — a 5th-century Coptic Christian site built from limestone blocks.
  • Red Monastery — another early Christian monastery with well-preserved frescoes.
  • Sohag Museum — showcasing artifacts from ancient to modern times.
  • Nile Corniche — a pleasant riverside area for evening walks and local dining.

Visitor Info

Sohag lies about 467 km south of Cairo and can be reached by train, car, or domestic flights. The best time to visit is between October and April, when the weather is cooler. Visitors are encouraged to combine Sohag with trips to nearby Luxor and Qena, exploring the ancient heritage of Upper Egypt.
