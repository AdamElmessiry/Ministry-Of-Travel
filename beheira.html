About Beheira

Beheira Governorate, located in the western part of the Nile Delta, is one of Egypt’s most fertile regions. Known for its agricultural production, particularly rice, cotton, and citrus fruits, it plays an important role in the country’s economy. Historically, it has been a crossroads between Cairo and Alexandria, making it both strategically and culturally significant.

Top Attractions

  • Rosetta (Rashid) — historic town where the Rosetta Stone was discovered.
  • El-Mahmoudia Canal — a vital waterway dating back to the 19th century.
  • Ottoman Houses in Rosetta — well-preserved examples of traditional architecture.
  • Edku Lake — a beautiful lake famous for fishing and birdwatching.
  • Local Markets — lively souqs offering fresh produce and handicrafts.

Visitor Info

Beheira is around 120 km northwest of Cairo and can be reached easily by road or rail. The best time to visit is during the cooler months (October to March), especially for exploring Rosetta and the coastal towns. Travelers should not miss sampling the governorate’s fresh seafood and traditional dishes.
