About Dakahlia

Dakahlia Governorate is located in the northeastern Nile Delta, with Mansoura as its capital. Known for its fertile land, Dakahlia is one of Egypt’s leading agricultural centers, especially for rice and wheat. The governorate also has a rich history, including the famous 13th-century Battle of Mansoura against the Crusaders.

Top Attractions

  • Mansoura Museum — exhibits chronicling the Crusades and Egyptian heritage.
  • Battle of Mansoura Memorials — landmarks honoring Egypt’s victory against Louis IX of France.
  • El-Mashaya Corniche — a lively riverside promenade in Mansoura.
  • Traditional Markets — vibrant souqs with fresh produce and handicrafts.
  • Rural Villages — showcasing authentic Delta culture and traditions.

Visitor Info

Dakahlia is about 120 km northeast of Cairo and accessible by road and train. The best time to visit is between October and April, when the Delta countryside is green and pleasant. Visitors can enjoy both historic Mansoura and the peaceful rural atmosphere of the villages.
