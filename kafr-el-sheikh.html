About Kafr El Sheikh

Kafr El Sheikh Governorate is located in the northern Nile Delta and is well known for its agricultural richness, particularly rice, wheat, and cotton production. It is also a hub for fishing, thanks to its long coastline on the Mediterranean and its access to Lake Burullus. The governorate blends fertile farmlands with fishing communities and historic sites.

Top Attractions

  • Lake Burullus — a protected wetland and bird sanctuary, ideal for eco-tourism.
  • Desouk Mosque — a revered Sufi shrine and an important spiritual center.
  • Burullus Fishing Villages — famous for traditional wooden fishing boats.
  • El-Farwa Archaeological Sites — remnants of ancient settlements near the Delta coast.
  • Rice and Cotton Fields — representing the agricultural wealth of the governorate.

Visitor Info

Kafr El Sheikh is around 140 km north of Cairo and can be reached by car, train, or bus. The best time to visit is in autumn and spring, when the weather is pleasant for outdoor exploration. Travelers can enjoy local seafood, explore eco-reserves, and learn about traditional agriculture and crafts.
